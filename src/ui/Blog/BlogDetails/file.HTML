In cloud computing, security is of paramount importance. AWS (Amazon Web Services) provides a system called IAM (Identity and Access Management) to protect your data and resources.

In this article, we'll discuss the basics of IAM, including the difference between authentication and authorization, a root user, IAM users, IAM user groups, IAM roles, and policies.



The difference between "Authentication" and "Authorization"

What is Authentication?

Authentication verifies user identity by validating their claimed identity. This involves challenging users to authenticate their credentials, like passwords, security question answers, or facial recognition. It occurs prior to authorization to safeguard protected resources and often utilizes an ID Token for secure transmission of user identity information.

What is Authorization?

Authorization establishes permissions and restrictions for system access, verifying access based on predefined policies and rules. It occurs after successful authentication, allowing only authenticated users with the necessary privileges to access specific resources. For secure transmission of authorization information, an Access Token is commonly employed.

To fully understand the difference between "Authentication" and "Authorization", consider the following analogy.

To enter the university building, a registered ID card is required, serving as proof of affiliation and authorization. It verifies your recognized status within the university community, granting permission to access the facilities. This example illustrates the process of authentication.


Once you have been granted permission to enter your selected university and college, understanding your role and responsibilities is essential. For example, as a university teacher, you have permission to participate in teaching activities and access student records. However, interfering in the university's financial affairs does not fall within the scope of your permit. This example exemplifies the concept of delegation, where specific privileges are granted based on an individual's role and responsibilities.
